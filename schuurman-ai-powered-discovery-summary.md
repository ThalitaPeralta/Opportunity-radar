# Robbin Schuurman — AI-Powered Product Discovery Summary

> Source articles published June 2025 on Medium

---

## Core Thesis

**Discovery is not a phase — it's a continuous habit.**

Most enterprise teams treat it as a one-time event (a sprint of interviews, a survey, a round of testing) then go back to shipping features. Schuurman argues this is the failure mode, and that GenAI makes continuous discovery *actually achievable* at scale.

---

## The Problem He's Solving

In large organizations, discovery is often skipped because:

- Nobody is formally asking for it
- Teams are in "delivery mode" — close tickets, ship features, repeat
- Research is delegated away from the building team, making insights delayed and unactionable

---

## How AI Changes the Loop

Schuurman's argument is that AI compresses the *effort* barrier that kills continuous discovery:

1. **Cluster incoming signals automatically** — support tickets, stakeholder Slack messages, email threads, bug reports — AI surfaces patterns without a researcher doing weeks of synthesis
2. **Turn feature requests into problem statements** — prompt AI to reframe "can we add X" into "what underlying need is X trying to solve"
3. **Simulate stakeholder perspectives** — ask AI "why is this frustrating for the business?" to stress-test your opportunity framing
4. **Summarize learning across sprints** — use GenAI (Copilot, ChatGPT) to roll up scattered notes into trends or emerging problem areas week over week
5. **Apply even during rebuilds/migrations** — if the original system was broken, rebuilding it as-is won't fix it. Discovery identifies which parts to question, adapt, or discard

---

## His Guardrail

AI supports product thinking — it does **not** replace it. Human judgment on what *matters* and direct customer contact remain irreplaceable. The risk is mistaking AI-synthesized summaries for actual customer understanding.

---

## Relevance to Feature Request Handlers

Schuurman's habits map directly to what a good feature request skill should do:

> **Ingest signals → Cluster into opportunity areas → Reframe as customer problems → Surface to the team for judgment**
