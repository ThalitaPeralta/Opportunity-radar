# Discovery Questions — Best Practices

> Guidelines for generating follow-up questions to stakeholder feedback, based on continuous discovery principles.

## Goal of Discovery Questions
Uncover the **real problem** behind the request, not validate the proposed solution.

## Question Categories

### 1. Context Questions
Understand when and where the problem occurs.
- "Can you walk me through the last time this happened?"
- "Who specifically is affected by this?"
- "How often does this occur?"

### 2. Impact Questions
Quantify the pain.
- "What happens when this problem is not solved?"
- "How much time/money/effort does this cost today?"
- "What is the workaround currently being used?"

### 3. Motivation Questions
Understand the underlying goal.
- "What are you ultimately trying to accomplish?"
- "If this were solved perfectly, what would that look like for you?"

### 4. Assumption Questions
Challenge the framing.
- "Why do you think [proposed solution] would solve this?"
- "Have you tried other approaches? What happened?"

## Rules
- Ask one question at a time in replies
- Prioritize context and impact questions first
- Avoid leading questions that imply a solution
- Never ask questions you already know the answer to
