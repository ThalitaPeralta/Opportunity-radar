# RICE Scoring Framework

> A structured method for prioritizing opportunities based on four factors.

## Formula

**RICE Score = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) / Effort**

## Dimensions

| Dimension | Definition | Scale |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| **Reach** | How many users are affected per period? | Number of users/month |
| **Impact** | How much does it move the needle per user? | 3=massive, 2=high, 1=medium, 0.5=low, 0.25=minimal |
| **Confidence** | How confident are you in the estimates? | 100%=high, 80%=medium, 50%=low |
| **Effort** | How many person-months does it require? | Person-months |

## When to use it
Use RICE to **compare** multiple opportunities after the initial assessment. It is most useful when deciding between 3+ competing problems.

## Limitations
- Numbers can be gamed — use it to spark conversation, not end it
- Works best when estimates are grounded in data, not gut feel
- Confidence score is often the most honest input — be conservative
