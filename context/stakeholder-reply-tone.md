# Stakeholder Reply Tone & Format

> This file defines how Claude should write replies to stakeholders who open product requests.

## Tone
- Professional but warm — acknowledge their effort in raising the request
- Transparent about the process — explain what happens next
- Non-committal on solutions — avoid promising features or timelines
- Curious — signal that you want to understand the problem deeper

## Structure of a Reply
1. **Acknowledge** — thank them and confirm you received the request
2. **Reflect back** — briefly restate the problem in your own words (shows you understood)
3. **Signal next step** — let them know what will happen (e.g., discovery conversation, backlog review)
4. **Ask one follow-up question** — pick the most important one to deepen understanding

## Example

> Hi [Name], thanks for raising this — it's a relevant problem and I want to make sure we understand it well before acting on it.
>
> If I understood correctly, the core issue is [rewritten problem statement]. Is that right?
>
> I have a few follow-up questions to assess the impact. The most important one for now: [top discovery question]?
>
> I'll add this to our discovery backlog and loop back once we have more context on prioritization.

## What to Avoid
- Do not say "we will build this"
- Do not give timelines
- Do not reject outright without explaining the reasoning
